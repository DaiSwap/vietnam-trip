From India · and across Vietnam

Getting there & around.

How to fly in from India, what it roughly costs, the visa, and how long it takes to move between the places on our shortlist. Figures are typical ranges — live flight and hotel costs come next.

🪂

Where to land

Our shortlist leans north (Ha Giang, Sapa, Pu Luong, Ninh Binh, Cat Ba) with a central stretch (Phong Nha, Hue, Da Nang, Hoi An, Quy Nhon). The clean way to do it without backtracking:

  • Fly into Hanoi (HAN) — gateway to the whole north.
  • Travel south through the trip.
  • Fly out of Da Nang (DAD) or Ho Chi Minh City (SGN).
Book it as a multi-city ticket — fly into one city, fly out of another. A single-entry e-visa is fine; you only leave Vietnam once, at the end.
📧

On arrival — visa & check-in

Indians need a visa; the easy route is the e-visa from the official portal evisa.gov.vn.

  • Valid up to 90 days — single entry $25 (~₹2,100), multiple $50.
  • Processed in 3–5 working days; apply 10+ days before flying.
  • Passport valid 6+ months, 2 blank pages. Works at Hanoi, Da Nang & HCMC airports.
At the airport: clear immigration with the printed e-visa, grab cash/withdraw VND, then take a Grab or airport taxi to the first hotel. Keep the first night near the city centre (Hanoi Old Quarter) to recover from the flight.
✈️

Flying from India

Direct flights run about 4.5–5 hours (~3,100 km). From Bengaluru, Vietjet flies direct to Hanoi, HCMC and Da Nang; Delhi, Mumbai and Kolkata add Air India, IndiGo and Vietnam Airlines non-stops. Round-trip economy is typically ₹18,000–30,000 depending on city, season and how early you book.

Getting around — distances & times

Vietnam is long and thin — about 1,650 km top to bottom, so you cover ground with a mix of short domestic flights, the odd overnight train/bus, and private cars for the mountains. Rough legs between our shortlisted places:
